Herbert Marcuse (1968) “One Dimensional Man”

The productive apparatus and the goods and services which it produces ‘sell’ or impose 
the social system as a whole. The means of mass transportation and communication, the 
commodities of lodging, food, and clothing, the irresistible output of the 
entertainment and information industry carry with them prescribed attitudes and habits, 
certain intellectual and emotional reactions which bind the consumers more or less 
pleasantly to the producers and, through the latter, to the whole. The products 
indoctrinate and manipulate; they promote a false consciousness which is immune against 
its falsehood…. Thus emerges a pattern of one dimensional thought and behavior in which 
ideas, aspirations, and objectives that, by their content, transcend the established 
universe of discourse and action are either repelled or reduced to the terms of this 
universe (pp. 26-7).

e.g., Apple - idol CEO - country - money

What do critical studies have in common?

All are in some sense oppositional to existing forms of society, of theory, of research methods etc.–for some, it’s a total war, making no compromises, taking no prisoners

  1. anti-empirical–a comfort-station for those fearful of statistics–Habermas once argued that use of number to study people is by its very nature anti-human and degrading

    斯大林：死一個紅軍是悲劇，死一萬個紅軍是數據

  2. anti-functional theory–accused of conservative status quo bias, although Stinchcombe used functional model to illustrate how power is maintained at various stages of history

    Conflict inside human body: on diet…

  3. anti-administrative research (Lazarsfeld)–policy implications are “tinkering with the system”

    御用文人：政治或商業用途

  4. anti-American–European origins
  5. anti-media centric–should start with a theory of society
  6. anti-micro-cognitive theory: individual change and immediate effects–Q. is individual data needed to test macro theory.

    階級論，人性論

  7. anti-consensus and social control—”balanced” forces of oppression

What is it in more positive terms?

  1. Relative importance of economic base– relation of persons in various social positions relative to resources.

    “You tell me anything, i tell you it’s ruled in money.”
    even friendship, like Song Jiang.

  2. Need to start with theory of society.
  3. Inherent overlap of theory and values-ideology:–need not just “understand” but to change society.

    在社會科學裡，客觀不一定得到，將由___代替。
    ans: intersubjectivity 互主觀性 different ppl share subjectivities, closest to objective acheivement.

    Still emphazise “Agreement”, “reliability”.

    Oxymoron!

  4. Importance of long-term consequences.
  5. Focus on conflict, contradictions within the system–power (e.g., contradiction between journalistic performance and requirement to show a profit in capitalistic society).

Weaknesses

  1. Polemical style of argument–hegemonic claim over good words.

    說不過你。

  2. Strong at diagnosis, but weak at therapy

    把脈診斷很好，但治病就不行了。

  3. Until recently, a lack of attention to the audience
  4. Hard to be even remotely “objective”–inter-subjectivity problem
  5. Hard to do well in terms of evidence–need for subtle coding of texts etc.
  6. Failure to achieve change (their goal: diagnosis vs. therapy)
  7. Internal squabbling–partly dynamic but also destructive

    內訌在不同學派之間。
