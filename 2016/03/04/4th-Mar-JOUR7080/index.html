Dealing with International Sources - JOUR7080

Posted on 2016-03-04   |   In Lecture

Courtesy and Acknowledgements:

Respect for cultures, politeness, and courtesy.

Minimize Metaphors:

Speaking metaphorically is a variable by generation, age, culture, and status.

Teenagers in China or HK may be quite different from other counties’.
We need different communication methods for different culture background.

Avoid Double-negatives:

Its very confusing and misleading to infer negatives with positives and vice versa.

e.g., Least worst. or, I don’t disagree.

Constants Really Count:

Enunciate Words Universally.

Do you run words together,
Do you blunt your T’s and D’s.

Missouri -> Missourah

Slow the Verbal Flow:

Increases interviewee’s confidence to communicate with you.

Do not Correct Mispronounced Words:

It compromises both the interviewee’s image and information.

We can ask them if happend:
Restate question in a different way. or Can you plz clarify that (for us in simple language).
If Not given the information I want:

I am listenning or ask the question again.

Lessen Ambiguity:

Words interpreted in two different ways are problematic.

Express Gratitude More Often:

“thank you” represents more than a casual courtesy.

Yes, I understand.
in some countries, “thank you” will lead to “hang out”.

Give them Ample Response Time:

Do not interrupt long pauses. Do not put words in interviewee’s mouth.

Flow with his/her idiosyncrasies and vocabulary.

Discard American/Chinese Idiomatic Expressions:

It might be offensive, funny, or utterly confusing.

Hey… What’s up? Have you got a piece(one organ)?
You’ve got beef(one organ)! Let’s grab lunch(sexual invitation in Italy).
pants in US -> trousers.

Be prepared to rephrase questions/comments:

Sturcture your questions and cross reference them.

Do not make American/Chinese Jokes:

American jokes are not even funny in China and vice versa.

Use Passive and Active Voices Wisely:

Although the active voice speech is most favored, some languages prefer the passive voice.

e.g., Trump has been a leader (passive)/ is a leader (active).
Avoid Figurative Speech, speak in Active Voice.

e.g., Japanese may say “yes” which means “no”.
https://www.ego4u.com/en/cram-up/grammar/passive

A Dress Rehearsal May Be Preferred:

It helps to make someone a conversational partner.

Help’s interviewee’s comprehension.
Correspondent gets detailed information.
Make them feel like friend’s talk and chat sth else first before the formal interview.

Must learn to manage temporality:

This is the urgent fact of tha deadline combined with the matter of regimentation.

Not all live breaking news from abroad is instant:

Consider the home time and the beat time.

Convey complexity through simple stories:

Use what I call information arbitrage!

e.g., Donald Trump relinquished his US (which should be specified) presidential candidacy at 6 p.m. (or yesterday) (can be written in “NY time on Thursday”)
